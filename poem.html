What the Canyon Holds

Ben Hickman · Canyonlands, 2024 · Free Verse

The light is not something you can hold,
only receive.

— Field journal, November 2023
I. The light arrives before the sun — a rumour of gold across the rock face, thin as a held breath, as the gap between asking and understanding.
II. We have no word for the colour the sandstone turns at five o'clock in late November. We say amber, we say ochre. We are wrong.
III. I set the shutter for twelve seconds. The light agrees to stay that long — no longer. When I look at what the sensor held,
I find I was not watching.
Note

This poem began as a caption and refused to stay there. It grew in the margins of a field notebook during three days in Canyonlands when the light was doing something I couldn't photograph — and so had to write instead. The final stanza arrived on the drive home, somewhere outside Moab, when I realised that the best frames I made that week were the ones I wasn't thinking about.
